{ CREATIVE TECHNOLOGY }

I ideate, prototype, iterate, and produce digital artifacts & interactive experiences.



Photo by Mary Inhea Kang

Hanul grew up in South Korea, near the ocean and mountains. "Hanul" means "sky" in Korean and is a pure Hangul word. Hanul spent most of their adult life in New York City, where they earned a BFA in Design and Technology with a minor in Data Visualization at Parsons School of Design and a BA in Culture and Media at The New School's Eugene Lang College of Liberal Arts. Hanul has a background in human centered design and engineering. Their research and development interests include accessible interfaces, emerging technologies, and generative arts. Hanul works as a product manager for a digital production company specializing in 3D automotive assets in Seoul.

Hanul's work ranges from AI-trained visual abstracts to data-driven infographics to mixed reality narratives. The software tools that they use are as diverse as the subjects of their work, and they are constantly learning something new. "Remember to imagine and craft the world you cannot live without, just as you dismantle the ones you cannot live within," said Ruha Benjamin, a scholar and educator. Hanul's creative practice entails critically re-interpreting the status quo, thoroughly re-imagining its content and structure, and re-constructing it in virtual, physical, and mixed environments. Hanul hopes that the process of creating various versions of the world helps us get closer to realizing a better world in our tangible reality.
Born in the 90s and raised in South Korea, Hanul witnessed the advent of digital culture, which quickly permeated many aspects of contemporary life. Hanul used to communicate with their friends on Korea's first social media platform Cyworld and read digital comics Webtoon, which is produced, distributed, and appreciated entirely online. As an enthusiastic participant of both, Hanul naturally developed an intimate understanding of innovative services that can create entirely new elements within our culture. Hanul is fascinated by how digital culture can so profoundly alter our lives. Hanul is always curious, and this trait has led them to do what they do now. The intersections of art, media, and technology inspire them.
